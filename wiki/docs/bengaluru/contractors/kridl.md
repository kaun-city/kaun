# KRIDL — Karnataka Rural Infrastructure Development Limited

## Identity

- **Registered name**: Karnataka Rural Infrastructure Development Limited
- **Also known as**: KRIDL, M/S KRIDL, Karnataka Rural Infrastructure Development Ltd
- **Type**: Government PSU (under Karnataka RDPR Department)
- **Phone(s)**: Multiple — 9845235505, 8904904737, 9449863065, 9845552355, 9845255243 (from BBMP work orders)

## Activity

- **Total contracts**: 100+ (across BBMP work orders)
- **Total value via Section 4(g)**: Rs 4,721 crore (without tender, per BNP analysis)
- **Total value overall**: Rs 13,000+ crore from BBMP (per Deccan Herald, March 2022)
- **Wards active in**: 198 wards (old BBMP boundary)
- **Period**: 2010-2025
- **Average deduction rate**: Higher than private contractors (quality issues documented)

## Flags

- **Blacklisted**: Yes — twice
  - **By BBMP (2010)**: Poor quality road work
  - **By Social Welfare Department (2018)**: Shoddy work, delays. Minister Priyank Kharge stated KRIDL was "taking a very long time to execute works"
  - Despite both blacklistings, continued receiving contracts via Section 4(g) of KTPP Act
- **Section 4(g) exemptions**: Rs 4,721 crore of BBMP work awarded without any tender process
  - Nearly 50% of 28,314 executed BBMP projects went to KRIDL
  - Karnataka High Court stayed the practice in 2021 after PIL
  - State government attempted legislative amendment to circumvent the HC order
- **CAG findings**: "Quality control system is non-existent". Rs 17,000 crore in unused funds
- **Lokayukta findings**: Probed 116 works, only 2 properly executed. Recommended KRIDL should no longer be entrusted with BBMP works
- **ED investigation**: Rs 12,943 crore in KRIDL works under ED scanner (January 2025)
- **Fake bills**: 8 BBMP/KRIDL engineers suspended for Rs 118.25 crore fake bill scam (June 2023)
- **Subcontracting**: KRIDL adds ~20% commission and subcontracts to private contractors

## How it works

1. BBMP issues a work order to KRIDL under Section 4(g) — no tender, no competitive bidding
2. KRIDL adds a 20% commission/overhead
3. KRIDL subcontracts the actual work to private contractors
4. Private contractor does 20-30% of the work
5. Bill is cleared for 80% of the sanctioned amount
6. Difference is distributed between the chain

## Notes

KRIDL is a textbook case of how PSU exemptions undermine public procurement transparency. The same government that blacklists the entity can bypass its own blacklist through the PSU tender exemption clause. The Karnataka High Court recognized this and stayed 4(g) exemptions in 2021, but the state government attempted to amend the KTPP Act to restore the loophole.
