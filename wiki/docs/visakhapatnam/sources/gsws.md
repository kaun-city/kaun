# Grama-Ward Sachivalayam (GSWS)

**The most ward-granular civic delivery infrastructure in any Indian state.**

## Portal

- **Main**: [gramawardsachivalayam.ap.gov.in](https://gramawardsachivalayam.ap.gov.in/)
- **Citizen services**: [vswsonline.ap.gov.in](https://vswsonline.ap.gov.in/)
- **Legacy**: [gramasachivalayam.ap.gov.in](https://gramasachivalayam.ap.gov.in/)

## At a glance

- **15,000+ secretariats** across rural (grama) and urban (ward) AP
- **1.5 lakh+ employees** working at ward/village level
- **500+ services** spanning 35 departments delivered through these secretariats
- Each urban secretariat covers ~50 households — ward-granular by construction
- Live attendance tracking, scheme delivery monitoring, household mapping

For Vizag, every ward has 5–10 ward secretariats. This is the densest service-delivery footprint in Indian local governance, and it's all visible.

## What's accessible

Through GSWS portals (some require lookup, none require citizen login):

- **Service status** — track any of the 500+ service applications by ID
- **Scheme eligibility** — whether your household qualifies for specific schemes (Rythu Bharosa, Aarogyasri, Amma Vodi, Cheyutha, etc.)
- **Volunteer / employee directory** — who's responsible for your secretariat
- **Household mapping** — every household geo-tagged to a secretariat

## What kaun.city pulls

Phase 1: **Ward → secretariat mapping** for Vizag's 98 wards.

Phase 2: **Scheme uptake per ward** — how many households are enrolled in major welfare schemes. This is genuinely positive data — surfacing which areas are reaching scheme saturation and which are under-served.

Phase 3: **Service request volume per secretariat** — how active each secretariat is.

## Why this matters

GSWS is one of the few governance investments in India where the digital infrastructure exists at the **household × scheme** level. Most "urban governance" data stops at the ULB. GSWS goes three layers deeper.

For a transparency platform, this means we can answer questions like "which wards in Vizag have the highest pension scheme coverage" or "which secretariat resolves housing applications fastest" — questions that have no equivalent in BBMP data.
